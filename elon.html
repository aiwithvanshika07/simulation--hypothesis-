ELON MUSK'S THEORY

Why Elon Musk believes there may be only a tiny chance that humanity is living in base reality.

The Famous Question

Elon Musk often asks a simple but powerful question: If technology continues advancing, will future civilizations eventually create virtual worlds that are indistinguishable from reality?

If the answer is yes, then there may be billions or trillions of simulated worlds compared to only one original reality.

According to Musk, this possibility dramatically changes the odds of where we ourselves might exist.

MUSK'S LOGIC
01

Pong To Modern Games

Forty years ago, games were simple pixels. Today we have photorealistic worlds, VR, AI characters, and massive online universes.

02

Future Technology

If technological progress continues for thousands or millions of years, future civilizations may create realities impossible to distinguish from actual life.

03

Billions Of Simulations

Advanced civilizations might run countless simulations simultaneously. Each simulated world could contain conscious beings.

04

The Probability Shift

If simulated worlds vastly outnumber real worlds, then statistically we are more likely to be inside one of the simulations.

Nick Bostrom's Influence

Elon Musk's idea is heavily inspired by philosopher Nick Bostrom. In 2003, Bostrom proposed a famous argument suggesting that one of three possibilities must be true:

  • Advanced civilizations never reach simulation technology.
  • Advanced civilizations lose interest in running simulations.
  • We are probably already living inside a simulation.

Musk believes the third possibility deserves serious consideration.

Musk's Personal Thoughts

Elon Musk has jokingly suggested that whoever runs the simulation might keep it active only while it remains interesting.

He has also said that advanced simulations would imply that civilization survived long enough to build them, which could actually be an optimistic possibility for humanity.

At times he has even mentioned feeling uncomfortable harming non-player characters in video games because it raises philosophical questions about consciousness and reality.

"The strongest argument for us being in a simulation is probably the following..."

— Elon Musk

Important Reality Check

Despite its popularity, simulation theory remains a philosophical hypothesis. No scientific experiment has yet proven that we live inside a simulation.

It remains an open question that sits between science, technology, mathematics, philosophy, and imagination.
