WHAT IS A SIMULATION?

A simulation is an artificial environment designed to imitate reality. The Simulation Hypothesis suggests that our universe itself may be a highly advanced simulation created by a civilization far more advanced than ours.

The Basic Idea

Imagine a video game character becoming conscious. That character would see buildings, people, cities, weather, relationships and history. Everything would feel real to them because it is the only reality they know.

Now imagine technology advancing millions of years into the future. Could a civilization create a simulated universe so realistic that the beings inside it never realize it is simulated?

That question is the foundation of the Simulation Hypothesis.

WHY PEOPLE BELIEVE IT

Technology Growth

Games evolved from Pong to realistic virtual worlds within decades. Future technology may create realities indistinguishable from ours.

Probability

If billions of simulations exist and only one base reality exists, statistically we may be more likely to live inside a simulation.

Consciousness

Some thinkers wonder whether consciousness itself could emerge inside an advanced digital universe.

"If reality can be simulated, how do we know ours isn't?"

Arguments Against It

There is currently no scientific proof that we live inside a simulation. Many scientists argue that a theory should be testable and falsifiable.

Others point out that simulating an entire universe might require unimaginable computational resources.

Therefore, simulation theory remains an intriguing philosophical possibility rather than an established scientific fact.
